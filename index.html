Intro

I am Sofía Binetti, contemporary dancer born and raised in Argentina, currently living in France. I carried out the majority of my studies at the School of Contempory Dance "Arte XXI" and the National University of Arts (Argentina).

My formation is based on:

  • Ballet technique
  • Graham, Müller, and floor techniques
  • Contemporary jazz
  • Choreographic composition

    • Complementarily, I train and teach the pilates mat and reformer methods which I firmly believe that are key tools for muscle development of dancers.

About

1

My first approach to dance was in 2012 when I was sixteen years old. I started taking jazz dance lessons with the teacher Sabrina Llado, who accompanied me for the following three years with her knowledge.

In 2014, I decided to increase my technique, so I started ballet classes at Julio Bocca Foundation. At the same time, I wanted to immerse myself in contemporary dance, by taking up floor technique lessons at San Martin Cultural Center. On the next year, I continued my formation at Domus Dance Academy where I took ballet, Graham and bio-mechanic stretching lessons.

2
3

In 2016, I started my contemporary dance performer career at Arte XXI School of Contemporary Dance under the direction of well-recognized choreographers and dancers, Oscar Araiz, Andrea Chinetti and Guido De Benedetti. In the next year, I entered the National University of Arts pursuing a degree in Choreographic Composition.

My entire formation since the beginning was accompanied by the knowledge of great and experienced teachers to whom I want to express my gratitude, Nira Bravo, Nadia Freedom, Stella Maris Isoldi, Alejandra Libertella, Paula Fontan, Sofia Ballvé, Ariel Caramés, Ayelén Clavin, Elena Ponce, Sonia Von Potobsky, Coty Rossi, Oscar Araiz, Anabella Tuliano, Sabrina Llado, Florencia Alonso, Juan Cruz Berecoechea, Lucia García Puente, Romina Bernardi, Silvina Duna, Andrea Chinetti and Guido De Benedetti.

5

My latest work as a performer in Argentina was called Entropía, directed by Aisha Rodríguez Palermo to whom I had the pleasure of accompanying and assisting during the process of creation. Entropía is a piece of contemporary dance with a 30 minutes-duration and 5 performers on stage. It was released on July 13th 2019 in Buenos Aires city.

I am currently enriching my dance studies in France and Luxembourg, where I have the pleasure of attending classes with internationally recognized teachers and choreographers.   At the same time, I started to be part of the Well Being Luxembourg team where I have the opportunity to give training classes by merging my knowledge in dance and pilates techniques.
